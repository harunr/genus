We’ve put together a Funding Guide to help you navigate the process of applying for a GENUS Seed Grant. It includes useful insights into eligibility, application requirements, and the review process.

Read Funding Guide

What type of grant does GENUS offer?

Who we fund

GENUS offers postgraduate bursaries (Honours, Masters, and Doctoral), postdoctoral fellowships, a research grant, and a seed grant specifically for public awareness programmes.

If I am not affiliated with a school, research, or museum institution, may I apply for a grant?

How to apply

No. The applicant has to be affiliated with one of our partner institutions. We do not award directly to individuals.

What academic, research, or museum partners are associated with GENUS?

Eligibility

The GENUS network comprises several key partners aligned to the development of Palaeosciences in South Africa. Our partners include Nelson Mandela University, University of Cape Town, University of the Free State, University of Pretoria, Rhodes University, University of the Witwatersrand, Albany Museum, Ditsong National Museum of Natural History, Iziko Museums of South Africa and National Museum, Bloemfontein.

How do I know if my topic is eligible for funding?

How to apply

GENUS supports research in Evolutionary Biology, Palaeontology, Palaeo-anthropology, Palaeobotany, Middle Stone Age Archaeology, Geochemistry, or other related disciplines to palaeosciences. If you have further questions, email a brief summary of your project to coe.pal@wits.ac.za
